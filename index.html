Meng Yu

I'm an assistant professor at Southern University of Science and Technology. From 2013 to 2016, I was a visiting assistant professor at Northwestern University. I received my Ph.D. from the University of Minnesota in 2013. My advisor was Tyler Lawson.

My research interests are in algebraic topology and related fields, particularly in its connections to algebraic geometry and number theory via objects such as formal groups, elliptic curves, and modular forms. A central theme in my research is elliptic cohomology and power operations, which combines the classical methodology of cohomological computations in algebraic topology with modern inputs from arithmetic moduli of elliptic curves in number theory.
